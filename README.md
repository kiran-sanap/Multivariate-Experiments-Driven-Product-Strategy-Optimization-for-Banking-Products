# Multivariate-Experiments-Driven-Product-Strategy-Optimization-for-Banking-Products
**Overview**

This project applies Business Experiments & Analytics and the Lean Six Sigma (DMADV) framework to redesign Fixed Deposit (FD) banking products based on customer preferences.
The goal was to identify reasons for declining FD satisfaction and validate a data-driven product strategy using real customer data.

- Region: Shivajinagar, Pune
- Academic Project: M.Sc. Statistics (OJT)
- Industry Partner: Pursullence Global Business Solutions LLP

**Problem Statement**

Customer satisfaction for Fixed Deposit products was declining despite strong performance in other banking services. The challenge was to optimize FD offerings to better align with changing customer preferences.

**Methodology**

The project follows the Lean Six Sigma – DMADV Framework:

1️⃣ Define

- Identified declining FD customer satisfaction
- Framed business problem and improvement goals

2️⃣ Measure

- Brainstormed FD features with banking professionals
- Selected 5 key features, each with 2 levels
- Designed conjoint questionnaires
- Collected data from 80 customers

3️⃣ Analyze

- Used Pursullence Analytical Software (Excel-based)
- Evaluated: Slope Coefficients (β), p-values, Statistical significance
- Identified most preferred FD feature combinations

4️⃣ Design

- Created an optimized FD product using best feature levels
- Conducted validation survey with 100 respondents
- Classified responses into Compliance / Non-Compliance

5️⃣ Validate

- Applied 1-Proportion Test
- Calculated: p-value, DPMO, Sigma Level
- Confirmed customer acceptance of new FD product
